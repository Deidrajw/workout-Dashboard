RDL back to 205 lbs

🟢 Add to programme
Nordic curl or GHD curl
Your hamstring work is almost entirely machine-based (leg curl). Adding one eccentric-heavy ham exercise (Nordic curl, GHD) would build the tendon resilience missing from your current programme — and directly reduces lower back re-injury risk. Twice a week at low load to start.
🟢 Add to programme
Progressive overload structure
Your current training is intuitive and high-frequency, but load progression is somewhat reactive. Try a simple 4-week wave for your key lifts: week 1 = 8–10 reps @ 70%, week 2 = 6–8 @ 75%, week 3 = 4–6 @ 80%, week 4 = deload. This is what will break your current plateaus (bench, lat pulldown).
🔵 Optimise

Shift one session to afternoon (3–5pm)
Your data shows 3–5pm produces the highest RPE (8.03) and strong volume. If you can move even one weekly session from your typical 5:30pm to 3–4pm, you'll likely see a measurable quality bump. Try it on Thursdays — your historically highest-intensity day.
⚪ Consider
Monthly self-audit
You now have 6 years of data. The single highest-leverage habit you can build is a 15-minute monthly review: sessions count, volume trend, top lift e1RMs, any pain flags. Catching a creeping volume spike early is how you don't end up in another Sep 2025 situation.

Return one pull movement to anchor status
Lat pulldown was at 125 lbs (2022–23) and is now at 110 lbs — below your old peak. Back training dropped from 17.5% to under 10% of sessions. One dedicated pull session per week (lat pulldown, iso row, face pull) would rebalance your upper body and support long-term posture health.
